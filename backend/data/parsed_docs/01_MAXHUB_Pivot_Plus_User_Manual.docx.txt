# Quick Start


# About Maxhub Pivot⁺

MAXHUB Pivot+ is a cloud-based CMS + DMS platform designed to centrally manage both devices and content across MAXHUB meeting room and display solutions.
It enables organisations to remotely monitor and manage devices while also creating, scheduling, and publishing content to commercial displays and meeting room screens.
Pivot+ provides a unified control layer for large-scale deployments, helping IT and operations teams standardise device behaviour, control messaging, and reduce operational complexity across locations.

## Key Features


### Zero-Touch Enrollment

Batch import device Serial Numbers and MAC addresses in advance. Devices will automatically complete enrollment once powered on and connected to the network — no manual binding needed, and they’re ready for use immediately.
The system automatically scans all connected Maxhub devices within the same local area network and lists basic device information. Managers only need to check the required devices to finish batch binding.

### Remote Batch Management

Intuitive visual charts and calendar views clearly show real-time device status, pending tasks, and Anomaly information. Help managers break free from cluttered data to focus on key details quickly.
Support remote control for multiple device types (including IFP, DS, LED, MTR, UC). Batch issue commands, push configurations, and install/uninstall/enable/disable software — with cross-timezone support for more efficient management.
The system supports up to 8-level grouping, allowing device management based on spatial concepts. Suitable for large multinational enterprises and chain retail scenarios.

### Efficient O&M

Supports simultaneous remote control of multiple devices in the same meeting room. When controlling one device, you can synchronously view the response status of other devices, making troubleshooting and collaborative O&M more efficient—no need to rush between different locations.
Firmware updates support both main devices and peripherals. Upload firmware independently for batch updates; the system automatically detects firmware versions and intelligently upgrades to the latest — keeping devices running at their best.
Custom hierarchical device alerting and full-dimensional log retention ensure fast response to Anomalyities. O&M processes are safe, controllable, compliant, and traceable.

### Flexible Content Distribution

Freely manage various resource types (images, videos, audio, documents, canvases, etc.), which are automatically filed into groups for easy management.
Enjoy flexible layouts when creating promotional content. Arrange various components and multimedia resources to meet promotional needs in diverse scenarios.
View-based content playlist arrangement and drag-and-drop time selection make content publishing more convenient and operations smoother.

# Quick Start Guide

In this guide, you can learn how to log in to the system, enroll your device, and learn how to navigate the system's menu structure and menu introduction.

## Log in


### Find your account

Check your email inbox for the email titled "Welcome to Pivot⁺" to find your account and your password information.
If you do not find a password in your email, it means you have registered an account before. If you have forgotten your password, follow the "Forgot Password" process on the login page.

### Access Pivot⁺

Click the button "Login Now" in the email to visit the Pivot⁺ website.
Or launch your browser and visit the following website:
https://pivot-plus.maxhub.com/
Then enter your email address account and password to log in.

### After logging in

Verify your organization name and your organization code (an exclusive 6-digit code) in the top left corner of the system.
If you have multiple organizations, you can switch to other organizations here for management.

## Install the Client

Before enrolling your device, please first ensure that the Pivot⁺ Client is installed on the device. The logo of our client is shown below—you can find it in the app list.
If the client is not available on your device, download it first. Via the System menu > Client Update > Client Logs and Downloads path, you can access the supported installation packages. After downloading the package, install it directly on your device to complete the process.

## Device Enrollment

Pivot⁺ provides multiple enrollment methods. Click the "Enroll" button at the top to select a method.

### Method 1: Manual Enrollment – Fill in Information Manually via Client

Find the "Maxhub Pivot⁺" client on your device and double-click to open it.
Click the "Enroll" button in the client, then enter the Organization Code and Administrator Account to enroll the device to your organization.
It is recommended to select a device group. After selecting a group, the device will automatically obtain and execute the group’s Profile configuration, and be added to the backend group for easier classification management.
It is recommended to enter a device name for quick identification and location of the device in the management backend.

### Method 2: Manual Enrollment – Enroll via USB Insertion into Device

From the "Enroll" pop-up window, click Enrollment File to download the enrollment file and save it to a USB drive.
Find and open the "Maxhub Pivot⁺" client.
Click the Link Device button, insert the USB drive. The device will automatically obtain the enrollment file information and fill in the Organization Code and Administrator Account. You only need to verify and confirm the information, then click the Enroll button to complete enrollment.
It is recommended to select a device group. After selecting a group, the device will automatically obtain and execute the group’s Profile configuration, and be added to the backend group for easier classification management.
It is recommended to enter a device name for quick identification and location of the device in the management backend.

### Method 3: Zero-Touch Enrollment – Batch Import with Pre-filled SN + MAC Information

Access the Remote Batch Enrollment function, download the enrollment template. Fill in the device serial number (SN), MAC address, and optional device group information in the template. After filling, upload the template to the system to complete pre-enrollment.
It is recommended to fill in the device group. After filling, the device will automatically obtain and execute the group’s Profile configuration, and be added to the backend group for easier classification management.
How to fill in device group information: If there is only one-level group, enter the group name directly; if there are multi-level groups, separate upper and lower level groups with 「/」, starting from the top-level group. Example: Group1/Subgroup2/··· (Group name supports up to 30 characters; up to 8 levels of grouping are supported).
When the device is powered on and connected to the network, the system will automatically identify the device’s SN and MAC information. If pre-filled information exists in the backend, the device will be enrolled automatically – no on-site operation at the device location is required.

### Method 4: Zero-Touch Enrollment – Auto-Detect Devices on the Same Network

Access the Auto-Detect Enrollment interface. The system will automatically collect network information of managed devices in your organization, and scan all connected Maxhub terminals (main devices with systems) on the same network.
Basic information of these devices will be listed on the interface. Managers only need to check the required devices to complete batch enrollment.
Q：How to Get the Organization Code and Administrator Account?
A：Log in to the Pivot⁺ Website. Navigate to the System > Organization page, then you can see the Organization Code and Administrator Account.
Q：How to Cancel Enrollment (Unenroll a Device)?
A：Go to the Device List page. Locate the device that needs to be unenrolled, then click Edit to unenroll it.
You can also select a batch of devices to perform bulk unenrollment.

Action Logs: Captures a comprehensive audit trail of all module operations, with clear visibility into user actions, timestamps, and content modifications to ensure operational traceability and security control.

# Device list


## Device List Overview

View enrolled devices, real-time online device count, and your organization’s remaining License credits. Perform group management, device detail management, command issuance, and other operations.

## Basic Device Information

View total enrolled devices, online devices, and License Credits at the top of the page:
Enrolled Devices: Total number of devices enrolled to your organization.
Online Devices: Real-time count of devices currently online.
License Credits: Remaining credits
1 License = 365 Credits
1 enrolled device consumes 1 Credit per day
License Credits = Number of managed devices × Management days (No limitation on enrollment devices)
Example: 10 devices for 3 years, the account will need 30 licenses(3,650 x 3 = 10950 credits).

## Device Group Management

Pivot+ supports up to 8 levels of grouping, ideal for complex management scenarios like cross-border operations or chain stores.
Hover over a group and click the Settings icon to edit the group info, add subgroups, delete the group, or edit devices in the group.
A single device can be added to multiple groups for easier management.

### Two Ways to Add Devices to Groups


#### Way 1: Manual Selection

Filter by device type, device time zone, or search by device name to locate target devices. Check the devices you want to add and click Confirm.

#### Way 2: Auto-Allocation

Only Admin or sub-admin can edit allocation conditions. After saving, devices that meet the conditions will be automatically added to the group.
Set conditions based on device name, model, OS, or device type.
Supported condition rules: Equal to, Contains, Starts with, Ends with.

## Device List


### Interface Introduction

View and manage devices in List View or Grid View:
Grid View: Check device screenshots. Click a screen to access the device’s detailed page.
List View: View more device parameters. Customize displayed parameters via Column Management on the right.
Device status indicators: Green = Online; Gray = Offline (may be disconnected from the network or powered off).

### Locate A Device

By Groups: Quickly switch groups via the left group structure to view group-specific devices.
By Filter: Filter by device type, system type, device status, time zone, name, or ID for fast retrieval.

### Quick Controls

Multi-Device Screen Control: Select one or multiple online devices in the same group to enable remote screen control (supports up to 5 devices simultaneously). While controlling one device, you can view real-time screen responses of other selected devices for more convenient O&M.
Screen control requires device permission. If the device user rejects the session, the IT administrator cannot take control.
If the user allows access or the device does not respond within 30 seconds, the IT administrator will gain control.
During control, you can operate the device desktop as if you were on-site. For Android devices, you can remotely disable touch functionality to ensure smooth control.
Export: Export device list documents or device Xbug logs.
Unenroll: Unenroll devices from the organization.

### Single Device Management

Edit: Modify the device name or unenroll the device.
Details: View basic device data and operation status.
Left Panel

Right Panel

### Send Commands

Select devices and Send device control, message, or settings commands.

Scheduled Execution: Specify a date and time. The system automatically sends the command, and the device executes it upon receipt.
Recurring Execution: Set custom execution days and time periods. The system automatically sends the command at the specified times, and the device executes it upon receipt.
After setting up a scheduled command, edit command plans in Command Records > In schedule.

# Profile


## Overview

A profile is a set of configurations and restrictions designed to manage and secure devices enrolled in Pivot⁺.
After setting a profile as a default, when a device enrolled to your organization, it will automatically receive and execute the default profile if no custom profile is matched.

## Create a Profile

Profiles can be created based on the device’s operating system (Windows, Android, Linux).
The configurable details vary by Device types—refer to the Device Compatibility List for specifics.
Note: "Default" in settings means no changes will be made—devices retain their original configurations.

## Device Execution

Associate Devices: After configuring profile parameters, click "Associate" to select devices or device groups that will execute the profile.
Automatic Execution: Once associated, devices will execute all profile settings immediately after each startup.
Execution Status: The list displays three statuses for each device
Success: The profile was successfully sent to and executed on the device.
Failed: Execution failed (e.g., system error, server timeout).
Executing: The device is offline (commands will resume when the device powers on) or conditional settings (e.g., Saving Mode) are pending trigger.
Available Operations
Sync: Immediately execute all profile configurations on the device.
Disassociate: Remove the device from the profile’s execution list (the device will no longer execute the profile after reboot).
Check: View detailed execution results for the device, including success/failure status and reasons for each parameter.

# App


## Overview

Batch install applications, enable/disable software that has run on devices in your organization, and uninstall installed software—all operations are handled remotely to boost management efficiency.
Supports sending software to devices for installation. You need to upload the software installation file or app store link first.
After the non-silently installed software is sent to the device, it needs to be manually triggered for installation on the device.
Support remote management of software installed on the device, including uninstalling installed software and enabling or disabling running software.

## App Installation


### App Upload

File Upload: Manually upload software in EXE, MSI, APK, or DEB format (maximum 600MB per file).
Google Play Link: Paste the URL of a free app from the Google Play Store (only free apps are supported).
App Installation List: All uploaded apps are displayed in the installation list. Supports re-sending, editing, or deleting installation packages.

### App Installation Process

Select the target app and specify the devices to send the installation command.
For free Google Play apps, enable "Automatic Update" to get automatic upgrades when new versions are released.
Installation Behavior:
Apps are downloaded and installed silently automatically (where supported).
For non-silent-supported apps, you can choose to push the app as a file to the device, then install it manually.

### Retry Mechanism:

If the device is offline or installation fails, the system will retry installation once the device is powered on and connected to the network within 7 days.
If the device remains offline or installation fails after 7 days, the command expires. Re-send the command or install manually with the device online.

### Execution Tracking:

View installation status in Command Records at any time.
One-click re-send is available for failed installations.

## App Management


### Run List

Displays all software processes that have run on devices in the organization.
Enable or disable app processes to restrict user access on devices.

### Installed List

Displays all software installed on devices in the organization.
Supports remote uninstallation of apps.

# Incident


## Overview

Incident automatically identifies Anomaly device behaviors during operation and notifies administrators by severity level. It helps administrators quickly grasp device anomalies and perform remote interventions.

## Configure Anomaly Detection Items


### Add Detection Conditions

Click "Add Condition" in the top right corner to select a detection item, set trigger conditions, severity level, and applicable devices. Enable the condition after configuration to activate it.
Multiple configurations can be created for the same detection item (for different devices or severity levels).
The system supports 12 detection items, each with customizable trigger conditions, severity levels, and applicable devices.

### Detection Items

Insufficient system disk space
Insufficient storage space
Excessive RAM usage
Excessive CPU usage
Excessive CPU temperature
Device offline
Device unenrolled
Unstable network
Client version needs update
Online devices idle for a long time
Device online for too long
Peripheral disconnection

### Trigger Conditions

Each detection item has fully customizable trigger conditions.
For example: "Excessive memory usage" can be set to "Alert immediately when memory usage exceeds 90% for 15 consecutive minutes".

### Severity Levels

Supports three levels: Normal, Urgent, Serious.

## Configure Notification Methods

Notification methods can be set separately by severity level:
Supported channels: Email and Info Center.
Personalized push: Targeted notifications for different roles.
Push timing: Immediate or custom schedule.

## Anomaly Identification, Notification & Remote Intervention

When an anomaly trigger condition is met, relevant administrators receive notifications based on configured settings and can perform remote interventions quickly.

### Notification Channels

Info Center: View anomaly incidents in the top right corner Info Center. Click any incident to view details.
Email: Receive Incident notifications via email.

### Remote Intervention

Anomaly details provided: Occurrence time, trigger conditions, resolution suggestions, and intervention options.
Intervention methods: Vary by anomaly type (e.g., shutdown, reboot, remote screen control).

### Anomaly Resolution

Manual resolution: Click "Solved" to close the incident manually.
Automatic resolution: The system continuously monitors. If the anomaly no longer occurs, it is automatically resolved and moved to the "Solved" list.

## Incident Summary Management

Access the Incident menu to view solved and active incidents.
Filter by incident items, incident type, severity level, device type, or device name to quickly locate anomalies for remote intervention or historical review.
Active: Click any unresolved incident to view details, including resolution suggestions and actionable items.
Solved: View historical records and details of resolved incidents.

## Client Download

Access "Client Logs and Downloads" to:
View update logs for Pivot⁺ clients compatible with various operating systems.
Download the latest client version directly for installation.

## Client Update

The device list displays each device’s current client version and update status.
Select target devices and click Update Client in the top right corner to initiate remote updates.

Select target devices and click "Auto-Keep Latest Version", set the update time, and configure options:

# WOL Service


## Overview

WOL (Wake-on-LAN) Service is required for remotely powering on Windows devices. Here you can learn how WOL works, download the WOL client, check WOL server status, and view detect results for whether devices can be powered on.
Once a Power On command is created, it is stored on the server. At the set time, the server sends the command to devices. Devices connected to the WOL Service will be powered on.

## Set Up WOL Service

Download and install the WOL Service. It can only be installed on Windows devices, and the device with WOL Service must be on the same network segment as the controlled devices.
Enter your organization code and admin email to connect the WOL server to your organization. Input a server name to distinguish different WOL servers in the same organization.
After connecting to the organization, the WOL server will start searching for nearby devices. The list of connected devices will be displayed in the WOL client and on the devices. One organization can have multiple WOL servers.
Ensure the WOL Service is always online—Power On commands are sent through this service.

## Server List

After WOL configuration, you can view the status of the WOL server and the number of devices on the same network segment in the "Server List".
You can also remotely delete the server via the "Delete" button.

## Power-On Support Detect

Go to "Service Detection" to detect if Windows devices can be remotely powered on. You’ll see reasons for failure (if any) and solutions. Follow the prompts to fix issues and retest.
To ensure devices can be powered on: Enable the Wake-on-LAN feature on the devices according to the method shown in the figure below.

### Remote Power-On Technical Principle

When a computer is shut down or in hibernation, its network card and part of the motherboard still receive weak power. This keeps the network card running at low power to monitor broadcast messages from outside the computer.
When the network card detects a specific "Magic Packet" in the broadcast and recognizes it as targeting its own address, it notifies the motherboard and power supply to start the boot (or wake-up) process.

##### Requirements for WOL Remote Wake-Up

The network card must support WOL or WoWLAN.
The motherboard must support WOL.
The power supply must meet the ATX2.01 standard.

##### About Magic Packet

A Magic Packet is a broadcast frame containing the MAC address of the target device. Since MAC addresses are usually unique, the packet is uniquely identified by the target device.
Transmission protocol: Usually UDP, with ports 7 or 9 (not mandatory—Pivot⁺ uses UDP but different ports).
Structure: Starts with 6 consecutive "FF" (hexadecimal, 11111111 in binary), followed by the 48-bit MAC address of the target device repeated 16 times. The total length is usually 102 bytes. Sometimes 4–6 bytes of password are added at the end.
